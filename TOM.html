The Basics

Tournament of Minds consists of 4 disciplines:

  • The Arts
  • STEM
  • Language Literature
  • Social Sciences

    • Teams are required to work together on a Long Term Challenge for six weeks without assistance from teachers, parents or peers. They are encouraged to explore possibilities and experiment with ideas as they endeavour to produce their best possible solution. They develop a creative and original way to communicate this solution to others, working within predefined parameters such as limited materials, complex challenge criteria and the deadline of Tournament Day.

    Students present the product of their ideas - their challenge solution - to a panel of judges and an audience on Tournament Day. They have ten minutes in which to present and must do so within a 3 metre by 3 metre performance area.

    The teams must also participate in an unseen Spontaneous Challenge on Tournament Day. This challenge requires rapid interchange of ideas, the ability to think creatively and well developed group cooperation skills.

    The Disciplines

    Arts

    The Arts discipline in Tournament of Minds (TOM) emphasizes creativity and innovation through various art forms, including visual arts, drama, music, dance, and performance arts. Teams are presented with challenges that require them to develop and perform a skit or presentation, incorporating storytelling, set design, costumes, and multimedia to communicate their solution. The discipline fosters skills in teamwork, creativity, critical thinking, and presentation. Judging criteria include originality and creativity, teamwork, communication, and artistic merit. This discipline encourages students to engage with the arts collaboratively and inventively, developing a wide range of valuable skills.

    STEM

    The STEM discipline in Tournament of Minds (TOM) focuses on science, technology, engineering, and mathematics. Teams are challenged to solve problems that require scientific understanding, technological innovation, engineering principles, and mathematical reasoning. These challenges often involve designing and building structures and creating technological solutions. The discipline emphasizes skills in problem-solving, teamwork, critical thinking, and creativity. Judging criteria include the effectiveness and innovation of the solution, teamwork, application of STEM principles, and the clarity and logic of the presentation. This discipline encourages students to engage with STEM concepts practically and collaboratively, developing skills essential for future academic and professional pursuits.

    Social Sciences

    The Social Sciences discipline in Tournament of Minds (TOM) focuses on understanding human society, relationships, and culture. Teams are presented with challenges that require them to analyze social issues, historical events, cultural phenomena, and human behavior. The discipline emphasizes skills in teamwork, critical thinking, communication, and research. Judging criteria include the depth of analysis, originality and creativity, teamwork, and the effectiveness of the presentation. This discipline encourages students to engage with social sciences in a practical and collaborative manner, fostering a deeper understanding of societal dynamics and developing skills valuable for future academic and professional endeavors.

    Language Literature

    The Language Literature discipline in Tournament of Minds (TOM) focuses on the appreciation and analysis of language, literature, and storytelling. Teams are challenged to explore literary texts, develop narratives, and create performances that showcase their understanding and creativity. These challenges often involve interpreting themes, analyzing characters, and constructing original stories or dramatic presentations. The discipline emphasizes skills in critical thinking, creativity, teamwork, and communication. Judging criteria include the originality and creativity of the narrative, depth of literary analysis, teamwork, and the effectiveness of the performance. This discipline encourages students to engage deeply with language and literature, fostering a love for storytelling and developing skills essential for academic and personal growth.

    My Experience with T.O.M

    I Have been competing in Tournament of Minds since I was 10 years old, the first time I competed my team chose the Arts and even though that was around the start of the pandemic we still had a great time, we didn't make it past Regionals that year. The next year TOM was canceled because of covid, despite this our facilitators (the teachers who orgasised it) decided to hold a mock competition for just our school, this time my team chose STEM. I honestly can't remember much about that year other than that we went into lockdown halfway through our challenge.

    The next time I competed I was in my first year of intermediate school, and this changed everything, for the first time we had acesses to a proper workshop, wood and most excitingly a 3d Printer. For our Regional challenge we chose STEM and as part of our solution we built a hologram box which lead us to win and have a shot at competing in the national final. At nationals we were presented with a new kind of challenge, previously we had six weeks to come up with a solution, write a skit and practice. but at nationals we only had three hours.
